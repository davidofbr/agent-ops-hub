## Link Quality Assessment

The link profile shows moderate strength with 22 referring domains and 25 total backlinks. The spam score of 39/100 is healthy—well below the concerning 50+ threshold—indicating the links are largely from legitimate sources. However, the Domain Rank of 9 is quite low, suggesting the site either has a newer or weaker backlink profile. The concerning aspect is that 59% of referring domains (13 of 22) are nofollow-only, which provides no direct SEO benefit and indicates reliance on low-quality or opportunistic link placements rather than earned editorial links.

The profile is well-distributed across TLDs with no extreme concentration. This is positive for a German-focused business, as the mix of .eu, .de, and .com reflects both local and international markets.

The profile is 60% nofollow, which is high and concerning. Only 10 of 25 links (40%) are dofollow, meaning most of the backlink equity is not being passed to the site. This suggests the links come primarily from:
- Low-authority directories or listings (often nofollow by default)
- Unvetted link-building campaigns
- Comment sections or user-generated content

This is a red flag for SEO value and suggests the site should pivot away from nofollow-heavy tactics toward editorial and earned-link outreach.

## Observations & Next Steps

- **Nofollow dominance is the primary issue** — 60% of links are nofollow, meaning the site is accumulating links with minimal SEO value. Conduct a manual audit of the top referrers and either disavow nofollow-heavy sources or focus on reclaiming them as dofollow opportunities.

- **Many "unknown" platform types suggest directory spam** — 16 of 25 links come from unknown sources. Investigate these referrers manually. If they're low-quality directories or comment spam, consider disavowing them to clean up the profile.

- **Link profile is fragile but clean** — With only 25 total links and 22 referring domains (high diversity), the profile is wide but shallow. There are no broken backlinks, which is good, but the overall authority is weak. Focus on earning links from high-authority blogs, industry publications, and German news sites rather than pursuing quantity.

- **Opportunity: German market underrepresentation** — Only 4 links from German domains. Consider link-building outreach to German-language blogs, industry associations, and local news outlets to strengthen local authority and improve domestic search visibility.

- **Before pitching a new client on link building, audit these sources** — The current profile shows signs of opportunistic link acquisition (high unknown platform, high nofollow). Use this as a case study of "what not to do" when designing a link-building strategy for new clients, or position it as a turnaround opportunity if the client is aware of past poor link-building practices.
